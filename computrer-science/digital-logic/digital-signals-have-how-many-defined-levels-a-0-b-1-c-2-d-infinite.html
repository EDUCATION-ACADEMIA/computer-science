Digital Signals have how many defined levels? a. 0 b. 1 c. 2 d. infinite

(A)

(B)

(C) **

(D)

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Common practical digital signals are represented as 8-bit (256 levels), 16-bit (65, 536 levels), 24-bit (16.8 million levels), and 32-bit (4.3 billion levels) using pulse-code modulation where the number of quantization levels is not necessarily limited to powers of two.

Concept note-2: -Digital signals and objects deal in the realm of the discrete or finite, meaning there is a limited set of values they can be. That could mean just two total possible values, 255, 4, 294, 967, 296, or anything as long as it’s not ∞ (infinity).

Concept note-3: -The definition of “signal level” of a digital signal is the level, expressed in dBmV, at which a sine wave would produce the same heating in a resistor as does the signal being measured.

Concept note-4: -In most digital circuits, the signal can have two possible valid values; this is called a binary signal or logic signal. They are represented by two voltage bands: one near a reference value (typically termed as ground or zero volts), and the other a value near the supply voltage.
