Which is a characteristic of a Type 1 hypervisor?​

(A) works within a host OS

(B) often used with client-side virtualization

(C) best suited for consumers, not businesses

(D) ** often called a bare-metal hypervisor​

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The most commonly deployed type of hypervisor is the type 1 or bare-metal hypervisor, where virtualization software is installed directly on the hardware where the operating system is normally installed. Because bare-metal hypervisors are isolated from the attack-prone operating system, they are extremely secure.

Concept note-2: -A Type 1 hypervisor runs directly on the underlying computer’s physical hardware, interacting directly with its CPU, memory, and physical storage. For this reason, Type 1 hypervisors are also referred to as bare-metal hypervisors. A Type 1 hypervisor takes the place of the host operating system.

Concept note-3: -When a hypervisor is installed directly on the hardware of a physical machine, between the hardware and the operating system (OS), it is called a bare metal hypervisor. Some bare metal hypervisors are embedded into the firmware at the same level as the motherboard basic input/output system (BIOS).

Concept note-4: -A Type 1 hypervisor runs on bare metal and a Type 2 hypervisor runs on top of an operating system.

Concept note-5: -A few examples of Type 1 hypervisors are Citrix/Xen Server, VMware ESXi and Microsoft Hyper-V. While considered efficient and well performing, these hypervisors are also known to be very secure.
