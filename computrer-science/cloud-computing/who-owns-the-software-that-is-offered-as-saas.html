Who owns the software that is offered as SaaS?

(A) ** provider

(B) customer

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The short answer is that you probably own the data you create. In most cases, you store your data in a cloud-based system, but the cloud service provider controls it. Your service level agreements (SLAs) define your company’s ownership and ability to retrieve the data on the vendor’s servers.

Concept note-2: -Software as a service (or SaaS) is a way of delivering applications over the Internet-as a service. Instead of installing and maintaining software, you simply access it via the Internet, freeing yourself from complex software and hardware management.

Concept note-3: -Who owns my SaaS data? In the vast majority of cases, you still own your data in a cloud-based system. Most service level agreements (SLAs) confirm your company’s ownership of your data located on the vendor’s servers, as well as your right to retrieve the data.
