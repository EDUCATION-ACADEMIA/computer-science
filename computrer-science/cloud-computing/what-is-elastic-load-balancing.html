What is Elastic Load Balancing?

(A) Automatically slowing down traffic to decrease latency

(B) ** Automatically distributing traffic across multiple targets

(C) Automatically monitoring traffic to ensure that costs are kept beneath specified threshold

(D) Automatically stopping traffic when targets are overloaded

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What is Elastic Load Balancing? Elastic Load Balancing automatically distributes your incoming traffic across multiple targets, such as EC2 instances, containers, and IP addresses, in one or more Availability Zones. It monitors the health of its registered targets, and routes traffic only to the healthy targets.

Concept note-2: -Elastic Load Balancing (ELB) automatically distributes incoming application traffic across multiple targets and virtual appliances in one or more Availability Zones (AZs).

Concept note-3: -Elastic Load Balancing automatically distributes traffic across multiple targets to ensure optimum performance in the event of a traffic spike. Some examples of these targets include Amazon EC2 instances, containers, and IP addresses.

Concept note-4: -A: No. For each associated subnet a Network Load Balancer is in, the Network Load Balancer can only support a single public/internet facing IP address.

Concept note-5: -A load balancer accepts incoming traffic from clients and routes requests to its registered targets (such as EC2 instances) in one or more Availability Zones. The load balancer also monitors the health of its registered targets and ensures that it routes traffic only to healthy targets.
