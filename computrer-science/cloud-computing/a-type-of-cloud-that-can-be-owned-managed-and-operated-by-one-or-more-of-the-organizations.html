A type of cloud that can be owned, managed, and operated by one or more of the organizations

(A) Public cloud

(B) Hybrid cloud

(C) ** Community cloud

(D) Private cloud

(E) All of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Community cloud is a cloud infrastructure that allows systems and services to be accessible by a group of several organizations to share the information. It is owned, managed, and operated by one or more organizations in the community, a third party, or a combination of them.

Concept note-2: -The private cloud can be physically located at your organizationâ€™s on-site datacenter, or it can be hosted by a third-party service provider. But in a private cloud, the services and infrastructure are always maintained on a private network and the hardware and software are dedicated solely to your organization.

Concept note-3: -The community cloud allows systems and services to be accessible by a group of organizations.

Concept note-4: -A hybrid cloud is a type of cloud computing that combines on-premises infrastructure-or a private cloud-with a public cloud. Hybrid clouds allow data and apps to move between the two environments.
