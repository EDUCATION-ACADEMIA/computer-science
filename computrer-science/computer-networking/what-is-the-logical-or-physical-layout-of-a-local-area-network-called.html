What is the logical or physical layout of a local area network called?

(A) setup

(B) ** topology

(C) configuration

(D) Channels

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A network topology is the physical and logical arrangement of nodes and connections in a network. Nodes usually include devices such as switches, routers and software with switch and router features. Network topologies are often represented as a graph.

Concept note-2: -A logical topology is how devices appear connected to the user. A physical topology is how they are actually interconnected with wires and cables.

Concept note-3: -Physical topology refers to the interconnected structure of a local area network (LAN). The method employed to connect the physical devices on the network with the cables, and the type of cabling used, all constitute the physical topology.

Concept note-4: -Logical Topologies. The two logical topologies are broadcast (also known as bus) and sequential (also known as ring). In a broadcast topology, all devices on the network receive every message transmitted.
