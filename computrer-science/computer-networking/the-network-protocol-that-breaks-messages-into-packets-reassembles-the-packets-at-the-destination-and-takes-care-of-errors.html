The network protocol that breaks messages into packets, reassembles the packets at the destination, and takes care of errors.

(A) Telnet

(B) ** TCP

(C) DSL

(D) UDP

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) guarantees reliable transmission by breaking messages into packets, keeping track of which packets have been received successfully, resending any that have been lost, and specifying the order for reassembling the data on the other end.

Concept note-2: -TCP/IP specifies how data is exchanged over the internet by providing end-to-end communications that identify how it should be broken into packets, addressed, transmitted, routed and received at the destination.

Concept note-3: -TCP is the component that collects and reassembles the packets of data, while IP is responsible for making sure the packets are sent to the right destination.

Concept note-4: -The Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) is a transport protocol that is used on top of IP to ensure reliable transmission of packets. TCP includes mechanisms to solve many of the problems that arise from packet-based messaging, such as lost packets, out of order packets, duplicate packets, and corrupted packets.

Concept note-5: -Some common examples of communication protocols include: TCP: TCP is an abbreviation for Transmission Control Protocol. This is a popular communication protocol that is used for network communication. It divides any message into a series of packets that are sent from source to destination, where they are reassembled.
