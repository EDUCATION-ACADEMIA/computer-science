COMPUTER NETWORKING

[SOURCES]
The type of computer networks used to connected computers within one building or not very far away from each other geographically

(A) Personal Area Network

(B) Metropolitan Area Network

(C) ** Wireless Local Area Network

(D) Wide Area Network

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A Local Area Network (LAN) is a network that is confined to a relatively small area. It is generally limited to a geographic area such as a writing lab, school, or building. Computers connected to a network are broadly categorized as servers or workstations.

Concept note-2: -Local Area Network (LAN) LANs connect groups of computers and low-voltage devices together across short distances (within a building or between a group of two or three buildings in close proximity to each other) to share information and resources.

Concept note-3: -Local Area Network (LAN) A LAN network connects a group of computers and peripheral devices in a specific area, such as a school, laboratory, home, or office building. This type of computer network is a popular way to share resources such as files, printers, games, and other applications.

Concept note-4: -LAN (local area network): A LAN connects computers over a relatively short distance, allowing them to share data, files, and resources. For example, a LAN may connect all the computers in an office building, school, or hospital.

Concept note-5: -A metropolitan area network (MAN) is a computer network that is larger than a single building local area network (LAN) but is located in a single geographic area that is smaller than a wide area network (WAN).
