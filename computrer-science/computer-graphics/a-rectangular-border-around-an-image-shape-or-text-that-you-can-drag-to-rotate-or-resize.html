A rectangular border around an image, shape, or text that you can drag to rotate or resize.

(A) ** Bounding Box

(B) Square

(C) Transform

(D) Clipboard

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A rectangular border around an image, shape, or text that you can drag to move, transform, rotate, or scale.

Concept note-2: -Go to the rectangular marquee tool, the little dotted rectangle near the top of the tool box. Drag a rectangle where you want the outline. 3. Go to Edit>Stroke (outline) selection and choose a color and width for the stroke.

Concept note-3: -When selecting multiple objects with the Move tool, check “Show Transform Controls” in the Options bar to display a bounding box around the selected objects, which will allow you to transform the objects by dragging any of the handles.

Concept note-4: -A bounding box is a temporary outline which highlights objects in creative software, such as Photoshop, 3ds Max, and Google Docs. Bounding boxes usually allow users to manipulate the object with transformation tools, such as scaling, moving, or rotating.
