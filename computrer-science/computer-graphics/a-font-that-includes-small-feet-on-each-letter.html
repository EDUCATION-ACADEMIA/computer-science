A font that includes small ‘feet’ on each letter.

(A) sans serif font

(B) ** serif font

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Serif fonts have little “feet” at the bottoms of the letters. You probably have Georgia and Times Roman on your computer, and those are both serif fonts. They are classic, timeless and make your business look established.

Concept note-2: -Serif fonts are the oldest, most basic fonts in the book. This classical type of font can be used in a variety of different ways and will likely never go out of style. This type of font is known by the “feet” seen at the top and bottom of each letter.

Concept note-3: -A serif is a small decorative line/shape that is added to a letter form. I sometimes refer to them as “feet”. Serif typeface refers to any font that has these embellishments.

Concept note-4: -Some of the most commonly used serif fonts include Times New Roman, Garamond, Baskerville, Georgia, and Courier New. Some of the most popular sans serif fonts on the black include Arial, Helvetica, Proxima Nova, Futura, and Calibri.

Concept note-5: -Serifs are the small feet at the end of the strokes on a letter or symbol. Sans is French for “without” and graphic design fonts fall into one or other of these categories (Sans/Serif) depending on whether those feet are present in the design of the lettering.
