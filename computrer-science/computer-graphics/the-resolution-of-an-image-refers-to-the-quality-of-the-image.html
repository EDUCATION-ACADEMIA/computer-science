The resolution of an image refers to the quality of the image.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Image resolution describes the image’s level of detail – higher resolution means more image detail. In digital imaging, the resolution is often measured as a pixel count.

Concept note-2: -Resolution is an important factor to measure the visual quality of digital images, photos and videos. A higher resolution signifies the picture contains more pixels, which means it can display more visual information. As a result, a high-resolution picture is sharper and clearer than a low-resolution one.

Concept note-3: -Resolution is the number of image pixels assigned to each inch when an image is printed-measured in pixels per inch (ppi). Thus, the more pixels an image has per inch, the greater will be its resolution. And, a high-resolution image will produce a better quality printed output.

Concept note-4: -Resolution represents the size of a media file measured in pixels, while quality refers to the details such as colors, shadows, lighting, etc. Most of the time, higher resolution means higher quality and that is the reason why resolution is so important!

Concept note-5: -WHAT IS RESOLUTION? The image’s resolution is the number of pixels divided by the size it is being viewed at-so if the image is 720 pixels wide by 720 pixels tall, and it’s being viewed at 10 inches square, it has a resolution of 72 pixels per inch.
