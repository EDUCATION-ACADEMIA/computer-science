Vanishing point is where

(A) Perpendicular Lines seem to converge

(B) ** Parallel lines seem to converge

(C) Origin

(D) Center of Projection

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A vanishing point is a point on the image plane of a perspective rendering where the two-dimensional perspective projections of mutually parallel lines in three-dimensional space appear to converge.

Concept note-2: -linear perspective, a system of creating an illusion of depth on a flat surface. All parallel lines (orthogonals) in a painting or drawing using this system converge in a single vanishing point on the composition’s horizon line.

Concept note-3: -Basic Perspective: Converging Lines It is easy to see diminution down a long hall or a railroad track because the converging lines go to the vanishing point. Converging lines, however, also occur within an object itself.

Concept note-4: -All lines that run parallel to the viewer’s line of sight, referred to as orthogonals, converge at the so-called vanishing point. The vanishing point falls on the horizon line.

Concept note-5: -Lines that are parallel in 3D inter-sect at a point in the image, called the vanishing point. Each set of parallel lines that is parallel to a plane will intersect on the vanishing line of that plane. The vanishing line of the ground plane is called the horizon.
