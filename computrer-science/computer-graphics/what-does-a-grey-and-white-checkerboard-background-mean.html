What does a grey and white checkerboard background mean?

(A) The image is broken.

(B) The background is gray and white.

(C) ** The background is transparent.

(D) The image will display a white box around it.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The ‘gray/white checkerboard’ is the general metaphor for transparent areas of a transparent imgage file.

Concept note-2: -Q. What does a gray and white checkerboard background mean? The image is broken.

Concept note-3: -A checkered background in Photoshop represents transparency in your project. Since the checked background is already transparent, there is nothing to be removed. To maintain this transparency for export, save your image as a PNG by going to File > Save A Copy and set the format to PNG.

Concept note-4: -Most images cover a certain number of pixels and have color in all of those pixels, even if that color is white. A transparent background has nothing in the background pixels, allowing what’s behind it to show through.

Concept note-5: -Look up any company logo followed by “transparent PNG” and you’ll see images with a white-and-grey checked pattern around them-that’s an indicator that the background has been stripped out (and no, those white-and-grey checks won’t show up when you use the design).
