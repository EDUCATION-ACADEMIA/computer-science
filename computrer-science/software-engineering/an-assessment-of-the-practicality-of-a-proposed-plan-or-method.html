(A) project management

(C) ** feasibility study

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A feasibility study is an assessment of the practicality of a proposed plan or project. A feasibility study analyzes the viability of a project to determine whether the project or venture is likely to succeed.

Concept note-2: -A feasibility assessment is a study to determine if the requirements of a project or a program can be met within the cost, schedule, and performance constraints of the project or program.

Concept note-3: -A feasibility study is simply an assessment of the practicality of a proposed project plan or method. This is done by analyzing technical, economic, legal, operational and time feasibility factors.

Concept note-4: -A feasibility study-sometimes called a feasibility analysis or feasibility report-is a way to evaluate whether or not a project plan could be successful.
