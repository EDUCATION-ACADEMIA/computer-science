SOFTWARE ENGINEERING

[SOURCES]
Model-driven engineering is just a theoretical concept. It cannot be converted into a working/executable code.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Model-driven engineering is just a theoretical concept. It cannot be converted into a working/executable code. Explanation: Model-driven engineering is an approach to software development in which a system is represented as a set of models that can be automatically transformed to executable code.

Concept note-2: -Model-Driven Engineering (MDE) is the practice of raising models to first-class artefacts of the software engineering process, using such models to analyse, simulate, and reason about properties of the system under development, and eventually, often auto-generate (a part of) its implementation.

Concept note-3: -Which of the following model in system modelling depicts the dynamic behaviour of the system? Explanation: Behavioral models are used to describe the dynamic behavior of an executing system.

Concept note-4: -Model Driven Architecture速 (MDA速) is an approach to software design, development and implementation spearheaded by the OMG. MDA provides guidelines for structuring software specifications that are expressed as models. MDA separates business and application logic from underlying platform technology.

Concept note-5: -Traceability is not considered in Requirement Analysis. Explanation: Requirements traceability is concerned with documenting the life of a requirement and providing bi-directional traceability between various associated requirements, hence requirements must be traceable.
