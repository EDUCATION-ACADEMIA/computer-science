When evaluating information, use the CRAAP principle, which stands for:

(A) Cost, Relevant, Authority, Accuracy, Purpose

(B) Currency, Role, Authority, Accuracy, Purpose

(C) Cost, Relevant, Authority, Accuracy, Perspective

(D) ** Currency, Relevant, Authority, Accuracy, Purpose

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -CRAAP is an acronym and stands for Currency, Relevance, Authority, Accuracy and Purpose. It provides you with a list of questions to help you evaluate the information that you find. You will be more confident in selecting sources to meet the expectations of your assignment.

Concept note-2: -CRAAP stands for Currency, Relevance, Authority, Accuracy, and Purpose.
