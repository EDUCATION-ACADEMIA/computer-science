Why is sequencing important?

(A) To ensure the algorithm is kept to a reasonable length

(B) To ensure the algorithm functions as intended

(C) ** To ensure the algorithm is efficient

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Why is sequencing important? It is crucial that the steps in an algorithm are performed in the right order-otherwise the algorithm will not work correctly.

Concept note-2: -This process is important because it helps to make sure that learners are able to build upon the skills and knowledge they have acquired, in a step-by-step manner.

Concept note-3: -An algorithm is made up of three basic building blocks: sequencing, selection, and iteration. Sequencing: An algorithm is a step-by-step process, and the order of those steps are crucial to ensuring the correctness of an algorithm.

Concept note-4: -Algorithms are well-ordered If the order is unclear, we may perform the wrong instruction or we may be uncertain which instruction should be performed next. This characteristic is especially important for computers. A computer can only execute an algorithm if it knows the exact order of steps to perform.
