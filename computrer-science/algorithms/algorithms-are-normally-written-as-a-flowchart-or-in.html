ALGORITHMS

[SOURCES]
Algorithms are normally written as a flowchart or in

(A) your thought process.

(B) Repeats a program

(C) Getting small part of a problem and adding them together

(D) ** pseudocode

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There are two main ways that algorithms can be represented – pseudocode and flowcharts .

Concept note-2: -Pseudocode is an artificial and informal language that helps programmers develop algorithms. Pseudocode is a “text-based” detail (algorithmic) design tool. The rules of Pseudocode are reasonably straightforward. All statements showing “dependency” are to be indented.

Concept note-3: -Algorithm and flowcharts help us to create new programs, specifically in computer programming. These tools are very helpful for programmers as they allow the programmer to analyze the entire programming process stepwise.

Concept note-4: -Algorithms are usually written in pseudocode, or a combination of your speaking language and one or more programming languages, in advance of writing a program.
