How much storage space do you need on your hard drive in order to install and use an application successfully?

(A) Just enough space to hold the program files

(B) Applications never use any hard drive space

(C) ** Enough space to hold the program files and any data files you might produce

(D) Just enough space to hold your data files

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If you want a hard drive for general use, your safest bet is getting a 500GB – 1TB hard drive for storing files. That should be enough to cover most standard file storage needs. The price difference between a 500GB and 1TB is typically negligible, so I personally recommend going for a 1TB drive.

Concept note-2: -You’ll commonly see a recommendation that you should leave 15% to 20% of a drive empty. That’s because, traditionally, you needed at least 15% free space on a drive so Windows could defragment it.

Concept note-3: -The Purpose of a Hard Drive The capacity of a hard drive won’t have any performance effect on your computer, unless the disk is nearly full, in which case it can cause some problems. If you have a 250GB hard drive, for example, it’s wise to leave around 10 to 15% of storage free.

Concept note-4: -Microsoft used the update to increase the Windows 10 installation size from 16GB for 32-bit and 20GB for 64-bit to 32GB for both versions. The drastic increase in size relates to an alteration to the Windows 10 update process.
