A ____ is a thick magnetic disk encased in a thicker protective shell. It consists of several aluminum platters, each of which requires a read/write head for each side.

(A) ** Hard Disk Drive

(B) Solid State Drive

(C) Optical drive

(D) Flash drive

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A hard disk is a thick magnetic disk encased in a thicker protective shell. A hard disk consists of several aluminum platters, each of which requires a read/write head for each side.

Concept note-2: -Hard disk drives (HDD) are thick magnetic disks made of aluminum platters, They use read/write heads to access data stored on the platters. They have up to several TB in storage capacity and their cost per MB is cheap.

Concept note-3: -A disk read-and-write head is the small part of a disk drive which moves above the disk platter and transforms the platterâ€™s magnetic field into electrical current (reads the disk) or, vice versa, transforms electrical current into magnetic field (writes the disk).

Concept note-4: -A hard disk is the primary computer storage medium, which is made of one or more aluminum or glass platters, coated with a ferromagnetic material.

Concept note-5: -hard disk, also called hard disk drive or hard drive, magnetic storage medium for a computer. Hard disks are flat circular plates made of aluminum or glass and coated with a magnetic material. Hard disks for personal computers can store terabytes (trillions of bytes) of information.
