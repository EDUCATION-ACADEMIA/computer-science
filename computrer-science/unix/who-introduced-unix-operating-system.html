Who introduced UNIX operating system

(A) Dennis Ritchie

(B) Ken Thompson

(C) Brian kernighan

(D) ** Both a and b

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In 1969 a team led by computer scientists Ken Thompson and Dennis Ritchie created the first version of UNIX on a PDP-7 minicomputer, which was chosen mainly because of Thompson’s familiarity with the system from his hobby work on it.

Concept note-2: -Dennis Ritchie and Ken Thompson’s creation of the UNIX operating system and the C programming language were pivotal developments in the progress of computer science. Today, 50 years after its beginnings, UNIX and UNIX-like systems continue to run machinery from supercomputers to smartphones.

Concept note-3: -Detailed Solution. UNIX is a computer operating system developed in the 1960s at the Bell Labs research centre and it was written in C Language. Ken Thompson, Dennis Ritchie, Brian Kernighan, Douglas McIlroy, and Joe Ossanna at Bell Labs were the developers of the operating system.
