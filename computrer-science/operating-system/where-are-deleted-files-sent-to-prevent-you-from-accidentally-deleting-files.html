Where are deleted files sent to prevent you from accidentally deleting files?

(A) ** Trash Can/Recycle Bin

(B) A folder

(C) They are automatically deleted

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Files that are moved to the Recycle Bin (on Microsoft Windows) or Trash (on macOS) stay in those folders until the user empties them. Once they have been deleted from those folders, they are still located in the hard drive and can be retrieved with the right software.

Concept note-2: -The files move to the Recycle bin.

Concept note-3: -The Recycle Bin is essentially a ‘last chance’ area for files that the user (you) wish to get rid of. To prevent the accidental deletion of critical data, the Recycle Bin requires a confirmatory step before the files are finally taken off your system.

Concept note-4: -Recycling Bin Its icon resembles a recycle bin or trash can, and the icon changes depending on whether the Recycle Bin contains any files. The recycle bin is a holding place for deleted files. To permanently delete a file, you can delete it again from the Recycle Bin, or you can empty the Recycle Bin.
