You just deleted a shortcut to an important work presentation. What happened to the data in the presentation?

(A) ** Nothing

(B) It’s in the Recycle Bin/Trash

(C) It’s deleted

(D) It’s in the recovery partition

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -You just deleted a shortcut to an important work presentation. What happened to the data in the presentation? Nothing. It’s in the Recycle Bin/Trash.

Concept note-2: -If you delete a shortcut from your desktop, have you also deleted the original file? No, because a shortcut does not place the actual file, folder, or app on the desktop.

Concept note-3: -As we know that disk formatting deletes data and removes all the programs installed with in the drive.

Concept note-4: -The close button terminates a window running on the window and it does not reduce a window on the screen in form of a button on the taskbar. The button that performs the minimization of the window in form of a button task is the minimize button.

Concept note-5: -Linux. Linux (pronounced LINN-ux) is a family of open-source operating systems, which means they can be modified and distributed by anyone around the world. This is different from proprietary software like Windows, which can only be modified by the company that owns it.
