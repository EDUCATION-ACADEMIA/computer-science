In SLR parsing for the grammar E’ → E, E → aEbE|bEaE|, In state 0, for inputs ‘a’ and ‘b’

(A) ** Both will have shift-reduce conflict

(B) Only ‘a’ will have shift-reduce conflict

(C) Only ‘b’ will have shift-reduce conflict

(D) Neither of the other option

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -SLR (1) refers to simple LR Parsing. It is same as LR(0) parsing. The only difference is in the parsing table.To construct SLR (1) parsing table, we use canonical collection of LR (0) item. In the SLR (1) parsing, we place the reduce move only in the follow of left hand side.

Concept note-2: -If you can construct a parsing table and there are no conflicts, it’s SLR(1).

Concept note-3: -False, LALR(1) parser is more powerful than SLR(1) and LR(0) parser. LALR offers a good solution, because LALR(1) grammars are more powerful than SLR(1), and can parse most practical LL(1) grammars.
