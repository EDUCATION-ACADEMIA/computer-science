E → E + T | F. Which is the correct answer after Eliminating Left Recursion

(A) E → FE and F → +T E | null

(B) E → TE’ and E’ → +T | null

(C) E → TE’ and E’ → +T | null

(D) ** E → FE’ and E’ → +TE’ | null

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A Grammar G (V, T, P, S) is left recursive if it has a production in the form. A → A |. Left Recursion can be eliminated by introducing new non-terminal A such that. This type of recursion is also called Immediate Left Recursion.

Concept note-2: -Left recursion is eliminated by converting the grammar into a right recursive grammar.

Concept note-3: -If you have left-recursion, then the parser goes into an infinite recursion. However, in right-recursion, the parser can see the prefix of the string that it has so far. Thus, it can check whether the derivation went “too far".

Concept note-4: -A grammar in the form G = (V, T, S, P) is said to be in left recursive form if it has the production rules of the form A → A |. In the production rule above, the variable in the left side occurs at the first position on the right side production, due to which the left recursion occurs.
