S → a | BB → Bb | EThe non-terminal ____ is left recursive.

(A) A

(B) S

(C) ** B

(D) E

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -With right recursion, no reduction takes place until the entire list of elements has been read; with left recursion, a reduction takes place as each new list element is encountered. Left recursion can therefore save a lot of stack space.

Concept note-2: -A Grammar G (V, T, P, S) is left recursive if it has a production in the form. A → A |. Left Recursion can be eliminated by introducing new non-terminal A such that. This type of recursion is also called Immediate Left Recursion.

Concept note-3: -Right Recursion-A production of grammar is said to have right recursion if the rightmost variable of its RHS is same as variable of its LHS. A grammar containing a production having right recursion is called as Right Recursive Grammar.

Concept note-4: -In the formal language theory of computer science, left recursion is a special case of recursion where a string is recognized as part of a language by the fact that it decomposes into a string from that same language (on the left) and a suffix (on the right).
