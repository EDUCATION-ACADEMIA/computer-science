EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A hamburger menu is an icon used on a website and in apps that, when clicked or tapped, opens a side menu or navigation drawer. It’s called a “hamburger menu” because it takes the form of the famous sandwich.

Concept note-2: -This is a hamburger navigation icon, and it is used to store all the navigation options. You can also call it a side menu or a navigation drawer, as it hides all your navigation features and will only open when you click on it. The hamburger menu icon has got its name because the design resembles a stacked burger.

Concept note-3: -The hamburger menu, or the hamburger icon, is the button in websites and apps that typically opens up into a side menu or navigation drawer. It was created by interaction designer Norm Cox for the Xerox Star personal workstation in 1981 as an easy way to communicate to users that the button contained a list of items.

Concept note-4: -Alternatively, you can press the hamburger menu icon from the top-left corner of your screen and then tap My plans option. This will bring the screen showing your existing plan details including the available SMS message, data, and voice call benefits.
