UNIX

[SOURCES]
Who were the main creators of the Unix operating system?

(A) Linus Torvalds

(B) ** Dennis Ritchie

(C) Bill Atkinson

(D) ** Ken Thompson

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In the 1960s and 1970s Dennis Ritchie and Ken Thompson invented Unix, arguably the world’s most important computer operating system.

Concept note-2: -Dennis Ritchie and Ken Thompson’s creation of the UNIX operating system and the C programming language were pivotal developments in the progress of computer science. Today, 50 years after its beginnings, UNIX and UNIX-like systems continue to run machinery from supercomputers to smartphones.

Concept note-3: -Thompson worked at Bell Labs for most of his career where he designed and implemented the original Unix operating system. He also invented the B programming language, the direct predecessor to the C programming language, and was one of the creators and early developers of the Plan 9 operating system.

Concept note-4: -The present owner of the trademark UNIX is The Open Group, an industry standards consortium.

Concept note-5: -Kenneth Thompson & Dennis Ritchie Develop UNIX, Making Open Systems Possible. . This was the first operating system designed to run on computers of all sizes, making open systems possible.
