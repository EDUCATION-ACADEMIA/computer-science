Both OR and AND gates can have only two inputs.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -5. Both OR and AND gates can have only two inputs. Explanation: Any number of inputs are possible.

Concept note-2: -Any number of inputs are possible in OR and AND gates. The AND gate is thus named because it behaves just like the logical “AND” operator. When both inputs are “true, ” the result is “true.” Otherwise, “false” is the output. In other words, only when both inputs, one AND two, are 1 is the output 1.

Concept note-3: -The AND Gate In case you might have been wondering, AND gates are made with more than three inputs, but this is less common than the simple two-input variety.

Concept note-4: -AND gate with one input simply means that all inputs are shorted together. If the AND gate has two inputs then it will function according to above truth table (only the yellow squared one inputs are eligible).

Concept note-5: -Most logic gates have two inputs and one output.
