In a clocked SR latch the outputs of the latch change when:

(A) Clock is 0

(B) ** Clock is 1

(C) Does not depend on clock

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -For this reason, having both S and R equal to 1 is called an invalid or illegal state for the S-R multivibrator. Otherwise, making S=1 and R=0 “sets” the multivibrator so that Q=1 and not-Q=0. Conversely, making R=1 and S=0 “resets” the multivibrator in the opposite state.

Concept note-2: -The circuit diagram of SR Latch is shown in the following figure. This circuit has two inputs S & R and two outputs Qt & Qt’.

Concept note-3: -So we will use this truth table to understand the SR latch as when one of the input is 1 the output of the NOR gate will be 0. So when S = 0 and R =1 the output Q will be 0 because the input of NOR gate G1 is 1.

Concept note-4: -S=1, R=1 is state forbidden in SR flip flop. The flip flop does not get damaged in forbidden state (S=R=1). It is called forbidden because there is no definitive gurantee of a fixed output.
