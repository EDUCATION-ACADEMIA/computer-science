What does GB stand for?

(A) ** Gigabyte

(B) Giantbyte

(C) Giagabyte

(D) Giggabyte

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A gigabyte (GB)–pronounced with two hard Gs–is a unit of data storage capacity that is roughly equivalent to 1 billion bytes. In decimal notation (base 10), a gigabyte is exactly 1 billion bytes. In binary notation (base 2), a gigabyte is equal to 230 bytes, or 1, 073, 741, 824 bytes.

Concept note-2: -The actual definition of 1 GB was always 1000 MB. GiB ( gibibyte ) is the one that means 1024 MiB ( mebibyte, also sometimes mibibyte ).

Concept note-3: -Quite simply, a gigabit connection exponentially delivers many more bits per second than a megabit connection does (1 gigabit=1000 megabits), just like a gigabyte contains exponentially many more bytes of storage space than a megabyte does (1 gigabyte=1000 megabytes).

Concept note-4: -What is a GB? A GB (gigabyte) is a way of measuring how much data you have on an electronic device. 1GB is approximately 1, 000MB (megabytes). The amount of GBs you have on your SIM plan determines how much mobile data you have available each month.
