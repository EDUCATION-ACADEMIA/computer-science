why the operating system has the minimum hardware requirements that must be met for OS?

(A) For the purposes of the customer’s computer to work faster

(B) To make it easier for customers to work

(C) ** For OS purposes can be installed and working correctly.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If your computer does not meet the minimum system requirements, the program you are attempting to install will not run and may not even install. If your computer meets the minimum requirements but does not meet the suggested requirements, the program is going to work, but it may be slow.

Concept note-2: -The published minimum hardware requirements exist for a reason. You might be able to install the OS on weaker hardware, but if you do, it’s not going to perform well.

Concept note-3: -Windows 11-64-bit 1 gigahertz (GHz) dual-core processor, 4 GB of RAM, 64 GB or larger hard drive, UEFI, Secure Boot capable, Trusted Platform Module (TPM) version 2.0, DirectX 12 or later with WDDM 2.0 driver, 9” HD (720p or greater) display with at least 8-bit color, Internet connection.

Concept note-4: -If the Operating system is not compatible with the computer hardware, the device can lose functionality or perform at a much slower speed than expected. The impact of the nonconformance is usually detrimental to the functionality of the computer.
