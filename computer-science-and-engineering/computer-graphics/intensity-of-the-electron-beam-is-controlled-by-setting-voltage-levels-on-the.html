Intensity of the electron beam is controlled by setting voltage levels on the

(A) Control grid

(B) Focusing system

(C) ** Deflection system

(D) Phosphorous

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Electron Tubes The beam intensity varies as a function of the beam-control element in the electron gun. In a TV or computer-display CRT, the electrical signal that controls the beam intensity corresponds to the desired picture information and is referred to as the video signal.

Concept note-2: -The deflection coils are used to deflect or tilt the electron beam off-axis.

Concept note-3: -With a voltage connected to the plates, the electrons experience a vertical force. The constant vertical force causes the beam to follow a parabolic path. This will be increasingly curved as the deflecting voltage is increased.

Concept note-4: -The horizontal deflection of the electron beam across the wider screens of larger TVs requires a high voltage (20-kV) at a frequency of 100 kHz [55].
