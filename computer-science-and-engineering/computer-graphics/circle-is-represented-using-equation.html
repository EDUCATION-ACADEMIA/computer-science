Circle is represented using equation

(A) ** f ( x , y ) = x 2 + y 2 r 2

(B) f ( x , y ) = x 2 + y 2 + r 2

(C) f ( x , y ) = x 2 + y 2 r

(D) f ( x , y ) = x 2 + y 2 + r

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The general equation of any type of circle is represented by: x2 + y2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0, for all values of g, f and c.

Concept note-2: -x2 + y2 = r2, and this is the equation of a circle of radius r whose centre is the origin O(0, 0). The equation of a circle of radius r and centre the origin is x2 + y2 = r2 .

Concept note-3: -The general equation for a circle is (x-h)² + (y-k)² = r², where (h, k) represents the center of the circle, r is the radius, and x and y form the coordinates of all the points on the circle’s perimeter.
