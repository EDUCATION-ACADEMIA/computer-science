If an image has a height of 2 inches and aspect ratio of 1.5, what will be its width

(A) 6

(B) ** 3

(C) 1.5

(D) 0.75

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When you know the width and want to find the height, divide the aspect ratioâ€™s largest number into the smaller and multiply the width by that number. For example if the aspect ratio is 1.5:1 divide 1 by 1.5 to get 0.667. If the image is 3 inches wide, 3 x 0.667 tells you the height is 2 inches.

Concept note-2: -2x3 aspect ratio: 720x1080 px is the most common 2x3 photo size, it is widely used in photography and printing.

Concept note-3: -An aspect ratio of 1:1.5 is equivalent to picture sizes of 10x15 and 24x36 | Picture frames, Custom picture frame, Canvas photo prints.

Concept note-4: -A 1:1 aspect ratio means that the height and width of your image are the same. You have a square. A 3:2 aspect ratio means that one side of your image is 50% longer than the other. A 3:1 aspect ratio means that one side is three times the length of the other. Comparison of aspect ratios of 1:1, 16:9, 2:1, and 3:1.
