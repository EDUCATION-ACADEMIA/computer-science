A bitmap or raster graph image is a grid of individual ____

(A) dots

(B) ** pixels

(C) bytes

(D) numbers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A bitmap is a grid of individual pixels that collectively compose an image. Raster graphics render images as a collection of countless tiny squares. Each square, or pixel, is coded in a specific hue or shade. Individually, these pixels are worthless.

Concept note-2: -A bitmap (also called â€œraster") graphic is created from rows of different colored pixels that together form an image. In their simplest form, bitmaps have only two colors, with each pixel being either black or white.

Concept note-3: -Rasters are a type of image that is stored in a computer as an array of tiny squares, or pixels. Pixels are the smallest individual element of a raster image, and each one can be assigned a colour.

Concept note-4: -A Raster, or what we generally call a Raster Graphic, is a computer image arranged in a rectangular grid of pixels or points of color.

Concept note-5: -Raster graphics are comprised of pixels. Pixels are small squares of colour, alongside each other arranged in a grid.
