Appropriate chart for visualizing the linear relationship between two variables is ____

(A) ** Scatter plot

(B) Barchart

(C) Histograms

(D) None of Mentioned

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In science, the scatterplot is widely used to present measurements of two or more related variables. It is particularly useful when the values of the variables of the y-axis are thought to be dependent upon the values of the variable of the x-axis. In a scatterplot, the data points are plotted but not joined.

Concept note-2: -Interpreting Scatterplots: Form Another important component to a scatterplot is the form of the relationship between the two variables. This example illustrates a linear relationship. This means that the points on the scatterplot closely resemble a straight line.

Concept note-3: -A scatterplot is a type of data display that shows the relationship between two numerical variables. Each member of the dataset gets plotted as a point whose x-y coordinates relates to its values for the two variables.

Concept note-4: -A scatter plot or scattergram chart will show the relationship between two different variables or reveals distribution trends.

Concept note-5: -The scatter plot is the standard way of showing the relationship between two variables. Scatter plots can also be expanded to additional variables by adding color, shape, or size to each point as indicators, as in a bubble chart.
