COMPUTER NETWORKING

[SOURCES]
Is a network that covers an educational or corporate campus. Examples include elementary schools, university campuses, and corporate buildings

(A) ** Campus Area Network (CAN)

(B) Wireless Local Area Network (WLAN)

(C) Local Area Network (LAN)

(D) Personal Area Network (PAN)

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A campus area network (CAN) is a computer network that spans a limited geographic area. CANs interconnect multiple local area networks (LAN) within an educational or corporate campus. Most CANs connect to the public Internet.

Concept note-2: -A campus network is a proprietary local area network (LAN) or set of interconnected LANs serving a corporation, government agency, university or similar organization.

Concept note-3: -Campus networks are normally interconnected with high speed Ethernet links operating over optical fiber such as gigabit Ethernet and 10 Gigabit Ethernet.

Concept note-4: -A campus area network (CAN) is a network of multiple interconnected local area networks (LAN) in a limited geographical area. A CAN is smaller than a wide area network (WAN) or metropolitan area network (MAN). A CAN is also known as a corporate area network (CAN).
