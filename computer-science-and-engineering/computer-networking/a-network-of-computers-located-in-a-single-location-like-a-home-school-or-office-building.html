COMPUTER SCIENCE AND ENGINEERING

COMPUTER NETWORKING

[SOURCES]
A network of computers located in a single location, like a home, school, or office building

(A) ** local area network

(B) wide area network

(C) metro area network

(D) wireless network

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A local area network (LAN) is a collection of devices connected together in one physical location, such as a building, office, or home. A LAN can be small or large, ranging from a home network with one user to an enterprise network with thousands of users and devices in an office or school.

Concept note-2: -A local area network, or LAN, consists of a computer network at a single site, typically an individual office building. A LAN is very useful for sharing resources, such as data storage and printers.

Concept note-3: -Local Area Network (LAN) A LAN network connects a group of computers and peripheral devices in a specific area, such as a school, laboratory, home, or office building. This type of computer network is a popular way to share resources such as files, printers, games, and other applications.

Concept note-4: -LAN (local area network): A LAN connects computers over a relatively short distance, allowing them to share data, files, and resources. For example, a LAN may connect all the computers in an office building, school, or hospital.

Concept note-5: -A Local Area Network (LAN) is a network that is confined to a relatively small area. It is generally limited to a geographic area such as a writing lab, school, or building. Computers connected to a network are broadly categorized as servers or workstations.
