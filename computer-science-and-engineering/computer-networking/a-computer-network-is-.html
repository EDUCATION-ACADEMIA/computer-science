COMPUTER SCIENCE AND ENGINEERING

COMPUTER NETWORKING

[SOURCES]
A computer network is ____

(A) Sharing of resources

(B) Communication from one computer to another computer.

(C) Exchange of data and information among users via network

(D) ** All

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Computer networking refers to interconnected computing devices that can exchange data and share resources with each other. These networked devices use a system of rules, called communications protocols, to transmit information over physical or wireless technologies.

Concept note-2: -A group of computers that are connected to each other for the purpose of sharing resources is called a network.

Concept note-3: -A computer network is a set of computers sharing resources located on or provided by network nodes. The computers use common communication protocols over digital interconnections to communicate with each other.

Concept note-4: -The simplest way of explaining the Internet is to call it “the network of networks.” It’s the connection of computer networks around the world into one entity, so to speak.

Concept note-5: -Two basic network types are local-area networks (LANs) and wide-area networks (WANs). LANs connect computers and peripheral devices in a limited physical area, such as a business office, laboratory, or college campus, by means of links (wires, Ethernet cables, fibre optics, Wi-Fi) that transmit data rapidly.
