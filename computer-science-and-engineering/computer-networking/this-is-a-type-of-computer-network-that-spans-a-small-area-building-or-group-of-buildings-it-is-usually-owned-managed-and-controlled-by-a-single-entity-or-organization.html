COMPUTER NETWORKING

[SOURCES]
This is a type of computer network that spans a small area, building or group of buildings. It is usually owned, managed, and controlled by a single entity or organization.

(A) Computer Networks

(B) ** Local Area Network

(C) Wide Area Network

(D) Wi-Fi

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A local area network (LAN) is a computer network that interconnects computers within a limited area such as a residence, school, laboratory, university campus or office building.

Concept note-2: -Local Area Network (LAN) LANs connect groups of computers and low-voltage devices together across short distances (within a building or between a group of two or three buildings in close proximity to each other) to share information and resources.

Concept note-3: -A metropolitan area network, or MAN, consists of a computer network across an entire city, college campus or small region. A MAN is larger than a LAN, which is typically limited to a single building or site.

Concept note-4: -A Local Area Network (LAN) is a group of computer and peripheral devices which are connected in a limited area such as school, laboratory, home, and office building.

Concept note-5: -LAN is a network that usually connects a small group of computers in a given geographical area. MAN is a comparatively wider network that covers large regions-like towns, cities, etc. The WAN network spans to an even larger locality. It has the capacity to connect various countries together.
