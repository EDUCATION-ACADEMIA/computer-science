Coaxial cable used in networking are usually terminated with which connector?

(A) RF

(B) RJ45

(C) Netbui

(D) ** BNC

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The most common type of connector used with coaxial cables is the Bayone-Neill-Concelman (BNC) connector (See fig. 4). Different types of adapters are available for BNC connectors, including a T-connector, barrel connector, and terminator. Connectors on the cable are the weakest points in any network.

Concept note-2: -Typical coaxial cable connectors used for CATV, satellite and video applications are called “F” connectors. Available in a compression, one-piece crimp, push-on, and a twist-on style. For security applications, BNC style connectors are used.

Concept note-3: -The BNC connector (initialism of “Bayonet Neill–Concelman") is a miniature quick connect/disconnect radio frequency connector used for coaxial cable.

Concept note-4: -Bayonet Neill-Concelman (BNC) coaxial cable connectors are one of the most commonly used connector types. They feature a twist and snap bayonet connection design that requires a quarter-turn to form a connection. Key advantages of BNC connectors include: Simple design.
