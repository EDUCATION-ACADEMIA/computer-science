EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Star or Hub topology is one of the most common network topologies found in most offices and home networks. It has become very popular in contrast to the bus type (which we just spoke about), because of the cost and the ease of troubleshooting.

Concept note-2: -Star topology: Star topology is the most commonly used topology system. Every node connects to a central network device in this layout, like a hub, switch or computer. Star topology is centralized in nature, making it user-friendly, reliable, and easy to manage.

Concept note-3: -Star topology is by far the most common. Within this framework, each node is independently connected to a central hub via a physical cable-thus creating a star-like shape. All data must travel through the central node before it reaches its destination.

Concept note-4: -The topology that is most useful for wireless networking at the moment is the Star, in which each node connects to a central hub that distributes the data wherever it needs to go. The most obvious example of this would be a home network.
