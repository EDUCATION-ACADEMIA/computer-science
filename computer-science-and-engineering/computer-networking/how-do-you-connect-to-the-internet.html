How do you connect to the Internet?

(A) ** through wired or wireless devices for a fee or for free using broadband Internet Service provided by an ISP

(B) you are automatically connected when you buy any device and charge it up or turn it on

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A wireless or WiFi network uses a radio frequency signal instead of wires to connect your devices-such as computers, printers and smartphones-to the Internet and each other. The WiFi signal can be picked up by any wireless-capable device such as a laptop or tablet within a certain distance in all directions.

Concept note-2: -Wired broadband refers to a connection that consists of cables. These cables can either be made of copper, fibre optic, or twisted pair. A wired connection is designed to carry different electrical signals from one end to another. In most wired networks, an internet connection is taken using a cable modem or a T1 line.

Concept note-3: -To establish connectivity, ISPs use various technologies-cables, telephone lines, DSL, satellite, fiber optics, and Wi-Fi, among others. Now, end-users must have a modem and an active account to establish a connection to their internet providers. Modems have cable outlets or a telephone line to connect to the ISPs.
