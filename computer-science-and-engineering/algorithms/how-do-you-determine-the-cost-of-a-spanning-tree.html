How do you determine the cost of a spanning tree?

(A) ** By the sum of costs of the edges of the tree

(B) By the sum of the costs of the edges and vertices of the tree

(C) By the sum of the costs of the vertices of the tree

(D) By the sum of costs of the edges of the graph

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sum of all of the edges in the spanning tree is the cost of the spanning tree. There can be many minimum spanning trees for a given graph. 3. Consider a complete graph G with 4 vertices.

Concept note-2: -The cost of the spanning tree is the sum of the weights of all the edges in the tree.

Concept note-3: -This can be generalized as weight = V2 – V + 1 where V is the total nodes in the graph.

Concept note-4: -Step 1: Determine the arbitrary starting vertex. Step 2: Keep repeating steps 3 and 4 until the fringe vertices (vertices not included in MST)remain. Step 3: Select an edge connecting the tree vertex and fringe vertex having the minimum weight. Step 4: Add the chosen edge to MST if it doesn’t form any closed cycle.

Concept note-5: -Theorem on characterization of an all-MST edge: An edge appears in every minimum spanning tree if and only if for any cycle that contains that edge, that cycle also contains an edge heavier than that edge.
