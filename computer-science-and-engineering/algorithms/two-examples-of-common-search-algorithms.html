ALGORITHMS

[SOURCES]
Two examples of common search algorithms

(A) ** linear search

(B) number search

(C) problem search

(D) ** binary search

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There are two forms of binary search implementation: Iterative and Recursive Methods. The most significant difference between the two methods is the Recursive Method has an O(logN) space complexity, while the Iterative Method uses O(1).

Concept note-2: -You can basically divide these algorithms into two different categories based on the type of search operations they perform: sequential search algorithms and interval search algorithms.

Concept note-3: -Examples include Dijkstra’s algorithm, Kruskal’s algorithm, the nearest neighbour algorithm, and Prim’s algorithm. Another important subclass of this category are the string searching algorithms, that search for patterns within strings.

Concept note-4: -Many people use binary searches from childhood without being aware of it. For example, when you search for words in a dictionary, you don’t review all the words; you just check one word in the middle and thus narrow down the set of remaining words to check.
