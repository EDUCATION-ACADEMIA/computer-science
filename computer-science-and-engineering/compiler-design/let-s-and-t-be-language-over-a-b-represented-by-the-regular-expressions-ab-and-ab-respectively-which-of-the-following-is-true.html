Let S and T be language over ={a, b} represented by the regular expressions (a+b*)* and (a+b)*, respectively. Which of the following is true?

(A) S ⊂ T

(B) T ⊂ S

(C) ** S = T

(D) S ∩ T ≡ ϕ

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Let S and T be language over = a, b represented by the regular expressions (a+b*)* and (a+b)*, respectively. Which of the following is true? Correct answer is option ‘C’.

Concept note-2: -Detailed Solution L = aa, aaa, baa, aaaa, abaa, baaa, bbaa … Every string in language is ending with aa. Therefore, it is ending with aa. Hence option 2 is correct.

Concept note-3: -Regular expressions are used to represent which language? Explanation: Regular expression is represented by regular language.

Concept note-4: -Which one of the following regular expressions represents the language: the set of all binary strings having two consecutive 0s and two consecutive 1s? Correct answer is option ‘B’.
