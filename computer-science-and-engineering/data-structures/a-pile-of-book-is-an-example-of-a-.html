A pile of book is an example of a ____

(A) ** Stack

(B) Queue

(C) Linked List

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A pile of books, a stack of dinner plates, a box of pringles potato chips can all be thought of examples of stacks. The basic operating principle is that last item you put in is first item you can take out. That is, that a stack is a Last In First Out (LIFO) structure.

Concept note-2: -Examples of stacks in â€œreal life": The stack of trays in a cafeteria; A stack of plates in a cupboard; A driveway that is only one car wide.

Concept note-3: -A stack is a linear data structure that follows the principle of Last In First Out (LIFO). This means the last element inserted inside the stack is removed first. You can think of the stack data structure as the pile of plates on top of another.

Concept note-4: -Stack is a container of objects that are inserted and removed according to the last-in first-out (LIFO) principle. Queue is a container of objects (a linear collection) that are inserted and removed according to the first-in first-out (FIFO) principle.

Concept note-5: -A stack is an abstract data type that holds an ordered, linear sequence of items. In contrast to a queue, a stack is a last in, first out (LIFO) structure. A real-life example is a stack of plates: you can only take a plate from the top of the stack, and you can only add a plate to the top of the stack.
