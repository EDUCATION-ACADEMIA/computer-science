Most Efficient Time Complexity of Topological Sorting is? (V-number of vertices, E-number of edges)

(A) ** O(V + E)

(B) O(V)

(C) O(E)

(D) O(V*E)

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Kahn’s algorithm is used to perform a topological sort on a directed acyclic graph with time complexity of O ( V + E ) O(V + E) O(V+E) – where V is the number of vertices and E is the number of edges in the graph.

Concept note-2: -The time complexity of topological sort using Kahn’s algorithm is O(V+E), where V = Vertices, E = Edges.

Concept note-3: -Explanation: We can implement topological sort by both BFS and DFS.

Concept note-4: -Kahn’s algorithm for Topological Sorting-GeeksforGeeks.

Concept note-5: -A topological ordering always starts with vertex whose indegree is zero and ends with vertex whose out-degree zero. Hence the correct answer is ABDC.
