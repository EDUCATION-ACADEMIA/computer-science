Gustafson Law’s (speedup) says that:

(A) Speedup is limited by sequential fraction of the program

(B) ** There are no any speedup limitation

(C) Speedup increase linearly with the numbers of used processors

(D) No correct answer

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Gustafson’s law argues that a fourfold increase in computing power would instead lead to a similar increase in expectations of what the system will be capable of. If the one-minute load time is acceptable to most users, then that is a starting point from which to increase the features and functions of the system.

Concept note-2: -Gustafson’s law and weak scaling If a problem only requires a small amount of resources, it is not beneficial to use a large amount of resources to carry out the computation. A more reasonable choice is to use small amounts of resources for small problems and larger quantities of resources for big problems.

Concept note-3: -The theoretical speedup of the latency of the execution of a program as a function of the number of processors executing it, according to Amdahl’s law. The speedup is limited by the serial part of the program.
