What does “Secure” means?

(A) ** Protected from danger or harm

(B) Protested by danger or harm

(C) Presented with danger or harm

(D) Provide information about computer data

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -free from or not exposed to danger or harm; safe. dependable; firm; not liable to fail, yield, become displaced, etc., as a support or a fastening: The building was secure, even in an earthquake. affording safety, as a place: He needed a secure hideout.

Concept note-2: -Secure means safe, protected. Your money is secure in a bank. Supportive friends and family make you feel secure. Secure can also be used as a verb.

Concept note-3: -This shows grade level based on the word’s complexity. to keep safe from harm or danger; protect; watch over: to guard the ruler. to keep under close watch in order to prevent escape, misconduct, etc.: to guard a prisoner.

Concept note-4: -to defend or guard from attack, invasion, loss, annoyance, insult, etc.; cover or shield from injury or danger.

Concept note-5: -“All Secure” is the call that goes out over the radio during a combat mission to signal that everyone is accounted for and that everything is ok, that the target is secure and threats have been eliminated.
