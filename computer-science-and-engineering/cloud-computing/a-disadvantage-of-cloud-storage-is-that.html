CLOUD COMPUTING

[SOURCES]
A disadvantage of cloud storage is that..

(A) Your data will always be hacked because your data can be accessed from any device with an internet connection

(B) ** Your data is vulnerable to hacking because your data can be accessed from any device with an internet connection

(C) Your data can easily be hacked because there is no system security provided by the host

(D) Your data is vulnerable to hacking because it is stored locally on your computer

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If your Internet connection fails, so does your access to remotely stored data. Internet failure thus leads to costly downtime when your business heavily relies on cloud storage. Furthermore if your Internet connection is slow you will need to wait a long time in order to access your remotely stored data.

Concept note-2: -The cloud is often seen as more secure than its on-premises equivalent, but it has its share of cybersecurity problems. According to the 2021 Thales Global Cloud Security Study, 40 percent of businesses say they have suffered a cloud data breach in the past year alone (Henriquez, 2021).

Concept note-3: -Downtime is considered as one of the biggest potential downsides of using Cloud Computing. The cloud providers may sometimes face technical outages that can happen due to various reasons, such as loss of power, low Internet connectivity, data centers going out of service for maintenance, etc.

Concept note-4: -Cloud based storage is dependent on having an internet connection. If you are on a slow network you may have issues accessing your storage.
