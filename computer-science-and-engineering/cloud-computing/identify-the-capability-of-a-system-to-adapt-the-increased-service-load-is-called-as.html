Identify the capability of a system to adapt the increased service load is called as.

(A) ** Scalability

(B) Tolerance

(C) Capacity

(D) None of the mentioned

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer is option 2. The capacity of a system to adapt to the increased service load is called scalability. The capacity of a system, network, or process to manage an increasing amount of work, or its potential to be expanded to accommodate that development, is referred to as scalability.

Concept note-2: -Explanation: Scalability is the capacity of distributed system to adopt the increased load.Distributed System.

Concept note-3: -Computers in distributed systems shares resources like hardware (disks and printers), software (files, windows and data objects) and data.

Concept note-4: -In a distributed computing system, a failure detector is a computer application or a subsystem that is responsible for the detection of node failures or crashes.

Concept note-5: -The opposite of a distributed system is a centralized system. If all of the components of a computing system reside in one machine, as was the case with early mainframes such as Von Neumann machines, it is not a distributed system.
