What happens to data when you stream a movie?

(A) ** It gets deleted as you watch it

(B) It is saved on your computer

(C) It is saved on the cloud

(D) It gets deleted after you watch the whole movie

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A primary difference between streaming and downloads is what happens to the data after you use it. For downloads, the item stays on your device until you delete it. For streams, your device automatically deletes the data after you use it.

Concept note-2: -The longer the film, the more data you use. The resolution you use also affects the amount of data you use. According to Netflix, you use about 1GB of data per hour for streaming a TV show or movie in standard definition and up to 3GB of data per hour when streaming HD video.

Concept note-3: -Watching videos in 360p for an hour will use only 60 megabytes of data. But watching videos in 4k for an hour might use 2.7GB of data on your plan or more if youâ€™re watching on a higher frame rate.

Concept note-4: -Data consumption is about 1 GB of data per hour when streamed on a smartphone, and up to 3 GB per hour for each stream of HD video on tablet or connected device.
