How do you access online accounts?

(A) ** Access via internet browser, then enter username and password

(B) Log into account

(C) Enter cloud website

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Type your administrator password into the “Password” text field, which is usually directly below the “Username” or “Email” field. Click the “Log In” button. It’s typically below the “Password” text field. Doing so will log you into the website’s administrator panel.

Concept note-2: -To check your credentials, Chrome first encrypts your username and password. Then it sends the encrypted credentials to Google for comparison against an encrypted list of known breached data. If Chrome detects a match between the encrypted sets of data, it displays a warning that prompts you to change your password.

Concept note-3: -In Windows, a local user is one whose username and encrypted password are stored on the computer itself. When you log in as a local user, the computer checks its own list of users and its own password file to see if you are allowed to log into the computer.
