If two dice are thrown together, then the probability of getting an even number on one die and an odd number on other die is

(A) 1/4

(B) ** 1/2

(C) 3/4

(D) 3/5

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If two dice are thrown together, the probability of getting an even number on one die and an odd number on the other is ? Explanation: The number of exhaustive outcomes is 36. P(E) = 1-1/2 = 1/2.

Concept note-2: -The probability of getting odd number is 21. The probability of getting even number is 21. The probability of getting one odd and one even in two dice throw is 2×21×21=21.

Concept note-3: -Answer: The probability of getting an even number on the first side when a pair of dice is thrown once is 1/2.

Concept note-4: -When a die is thrown, getting an even number. ∴ The probability of getting an even number when a die is thrown is 1/2.
