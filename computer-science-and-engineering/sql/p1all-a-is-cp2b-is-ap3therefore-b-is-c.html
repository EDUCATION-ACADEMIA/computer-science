(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Types of Syllogism There are three main types of syllogisms each with distinct qualities: conditional, categorical, and disjunctive.

Concept note-2: -are propositions: For example, if someone is going to study law or medicine, and does not study law, they will therefore study medicine.

Concept note-3: -All terriers are dogs. All terriers are mammals. Therefore, All mammals are dogs. Both premises of this syllogism are true, while its conclusion is false, so it is clearly invalid.

Concept note-4: -For example: Major Premise: “All philosophers are thinkers.” Minor Premise: “Some philosophers are teachers.” Conclusion: “Some thinkers are teachers.”
