Which of the statements below refers to Warm Boot?

(A) Turning on computer that has been powered off

(B) ** Restarting computer that is powered on

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Warm boot refers to restarting a computer that is already turned on via the operating system. Restarting it returns the computer to its initial state. A warm boot is sometimes necessary when a program encounters an error from which it cannot recover.

Concept note-2: -Techopedia Explains Warm Reboot Typically, a warm boot is performed by pressing the CTRL-ALT-DEL key simultaneously within Windows OS or selecting the restart function from the Start menu. In warm a reboot, the computer reloads faster than the time it would take to revive it from cold or dead state.

Concept note-3: -A warm boot (also called a “soft boot") is the process of restarting a computer. It may be used in contrast to a cold boot, which refers to starting up a computer that has been turned off. Warm boots are typically initiated by a “Restart” command in the operating system.

Concept note-4: -You can also perform a warm boot by selecting “Restart” from the menu bar and hitting the button. After a few seconds, your computer will eventually go back to where it was initially and finish the process.
