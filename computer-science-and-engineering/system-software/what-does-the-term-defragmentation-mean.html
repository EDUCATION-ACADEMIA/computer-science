What does the term” Defragmentation” mean?

(A) ** Putting the fragmented data together

(B) Makes space on the HDD by getting rid of any unused

(C) Scans your computer for viruses.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Defragmentation is the process of locating the noncontiguous fragments of data into which a computer file may be divided as it is stored on a hard disk, and rearranging the fragments and restoring them into fewer fragments or into the whole file.

Concept note-2: -Defragmentation organizes storage on your computer by consolidating files and other data saved on your hard drive. When there’s not enough space on your disk to store an entire file in one place, the file is broken down into smaller pieces called fragments. Defragmentation puts those pieces back together.

Concept note-3: -Modern “Defrag” The most popular “disk defragmenter” of all time is Diskeeper.

Concept note-4: -A fragmented disk takes longer to read from and write to, making a computer run slower. Defragmentation software takes the fragmented files and rearranges the segments so that they run contiguously. This decreases read/write time, thereby speeding up computer performance.
