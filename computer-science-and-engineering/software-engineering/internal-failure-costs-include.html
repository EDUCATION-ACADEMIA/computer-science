SOFTWARE ENGINEERING

[SOURCES]
Internal failure costs include

(A) a)rework

(B) b)repair

(C) c)failure mode analysis

(D) ** All a, b, c

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Internal failure costs They could include: Waste: Performance of unnecessary work or holding of stock as a result of errors, poor organization, or communication. Scrap: Defective product or material that cannot be repaired, used, or sold. Rework or rectification: Correction of defective material or errors.

Concept note-2: -There are two types of failure costs, which are internal and external. Internal failure costs occur before goods are shipped to customers, while external failure costs arise subsequent to shipment.

Concept note-3: -Internal failure describes a product or process that will not pass final inspection and is usually scrapped or reworked.

Concept note-4: -9. Which of the following does not belong to Internal Failure Costs? Explanation: Rework, Scrap and Design Corrective Action fall under the category of Internal Failure Costs. Returned Goods fall under the category of External Failure Costs.
