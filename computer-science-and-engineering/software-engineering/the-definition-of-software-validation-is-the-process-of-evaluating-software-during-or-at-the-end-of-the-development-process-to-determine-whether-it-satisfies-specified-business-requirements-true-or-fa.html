The definition of software validation is the process of evaluating software during or at the end of the development process to determine whether it satisfies specified business requirements. True or false?

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Definition: The process of evaluating software during or at the end of the development process to determine whether it satisfies specified requirements. Validation is the process of evaluating the final product to check whether the software meets the customer expectations and requirements.

Concept note-2: -Validation testing is the process of assessing a new software product to ensure that its performance matches consumer needs. Product development teams might perform validation testing to learn about the integrity of the product itself and its performance in different environments.

Concept note-3: -Verification is the process of evaluating products of a development phase to find out whether they meet the specified requirements.

Concept note-4: -Validation is the process of determining whether or not a software product is up to par, or in other words, whether or not the product meets high-level specifications. Here, you will find the MCQs on Verification and Validation in Software Engineering.
