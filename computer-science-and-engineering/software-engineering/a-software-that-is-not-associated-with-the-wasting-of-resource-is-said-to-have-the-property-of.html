A software that is NOT associated with the wasting of resource is said to have the property of

(A) reliability

(B) ** efficiency

(C) dependability

(D) usability

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Resource efficiency means using the Earthâ€™s limited resources in a sustainable manner while minimising impacts on the environment. Resource efficiency includes for example re-manufacturing, repair, maintenance, recycling and eco-design.

Concept note-2: -Many factors determine resource use and productivity, including climate, population density, infrastructure needs, domestic availability of raw materials versus reliance on imports, prevailing fuel in the power generation sector, the rate of economic growth, technological development, and the structure of the economy.

Concept note-3: -The term efficiency can be defined as the ability to achieve an end goal with little to no waste, effort, or energy. Being efficient means you can achieve your results by putting the resources you have in the best way possible. Put simply, something is efficient if nothing is wasted and all processes are optimized.

Concept note-4: -Efficiency is when you do the same things faster or with less waste. For example, you might find a better way to run a project status meeting so it takes 30 minutes on average rather than 45 minutes. Effectiveness is finding a way to improve outcomes, perhaps by doing something very differently.
